# Konstantina Freri Portfolio

This is my personal portfolio website, where I present selected work across software development, design, mobile experiences, and human-centered technology. I wanted it to feel like more than a list of projects, so I built it as a small guided experience that introduces who I am, how I work, and what kinds of problems I enjoy solving.

The site is built with React, TypeScript, and Vite, with motion and gallery interactions that help the project stories feel visual and easy to explore without becoming overly technical.

## Overview

I organized the portfolio into a few main sections:

- Intro: a short personal introduction with animated highlights that reflect the kind of work I care about
- Projects: a curated set of case studies with context, visuals, and feature breakdowns
- About: my professional experience, education, and core skills
- More About: a more personal section that shows a bit of who I am outside of work
- Contact: quick ways to reach me through email, LinkedIn, and GitHub

## Featured Projects

The portfolio currently features five projects, each representing a different part of my background:

- NNEDS: work from my time as a frontend developer at Novo Nordisk, where I helped improve a data platform through user support tools, annotation features, interface redesign, and role-based access control.
- Ambulance Adventure: my Master's thesis project, a physical interactive board game designed to help reduce anxiety for children visiting a pediatric emergency department.
- Moodle: my diploma thesis project, an Android app that analyzes a user's facial expression and creates a Spotify playlist that matches their mood.
- Check It: a mobile app focused on daily goals, habits, and mood tracking, designed to help users reflect on the relationship between achievement and emotional well-being.
- Yellow Pages: a team project created for a venture capital context, where I contributed to the design and frontend of a web app for exploring and filtering professional network connections.

## What I Wanted This Portfolio To Show

Across these projects, I wanted to highlight:

- my interest in user-centered and meaningful digital experiences
- a mix of web, mobile, and physical product work
- the balance I try to keep between design thinking and implementation
- the way I use visuals and storytelling to explain process and outcomes

## Project Structure

The project itself is fairly simple and intentionally focused:

- the main app brings together the intro, projects, about, personal, and contact sections
- project content is driven by structured data so each case study can be presented consistently
- images in the public folder support the project galleries and hobbies section
- reusable components handle things like navigation, project presentation, hover effects, galleries, and contact links

## Design Direction

I wanted the site to feel warm, expressive, and personal rather than formal or corporate. The goal was to create something that reads like a curated introduction to my work, while still being easy to scan for someone who wants to quickly understand my experience and interests.
